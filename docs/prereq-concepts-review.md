## Verifier reports (verbatim assessments)

**Coverage verifier:** Coverage is strong: of the 230 UC receiving courses, all math (calc 1-3, linear algebra, ODE, discrete, business calc), physics (calc-based mech/E&M/waves-thermo/modern plus UCSB's algebra-based 6-series), chemistry (gen chem incl. third-quarter courses like CHEM 1C/CHE 002C mapping to gen_chem_2, organic chem with labs), biology (cell/molecular, organismal incl. BIS 002C, human physiology), CS (CS1/CS2/CS3, computer organization, C/systems, discrete), engineering (circuits incl. EECS 70A/ENG 017/ENGR 065 and plausibly EECS 16A/B, digital logic, MATLAB-for-engineers), and the humanities/social-science set (comp 1, public speaking, micro/macro econ, financial accounting, intro lit, world lit I/II) have a plausible concept. Lab-only courses (PHYSICS 4AL/4BL, 7LC/7LD, PHYS 3L/6AL-6CL/7L, CHEM 1AL/2AL/2BL/002L, BIOLOGY 1AL, MCDB 1LL, BILD 4, MCELLBI 32L) are implicitly covered by their lecture concepts, consistent with the vocabulary's own practice of anchoring CHEM 3AL/3BL under organic_chem — acceptable, but worth stating as an explicit convention. The one recurring true gap is the software-tools/construction cluster (COM SCI 35L, CSE 15L, IN4MATX 43). Two anchor-integrity defects: (1) comp_arch_assembly cites 'CSE 030 @ UC Merced', but per the dataset CSE 030 is 'Data Structures' — the computer-organization course is CSE 031, so this anchor is misassigned (CSE 030 belongs under cs_3_data_structures); (2) intro_stats and stats_calc share the identical anchors STATS 67 @ UCI and MATH 032 @ UC Merced — a CC algebra-based stats course and a calc-based one cannot both anchor to the same UC courses, leaving intro_stats without independent justification; engl_comp_2 has an explicitly empty anchor list. Recommend: add a software-tools concept (or document why it is out of scope), fix the CSE 030→031 anchor, and either find a genuine algebra-based UC anchor for intro_stats or merge/justify it and engl_comp_2 as CC-side-requirement concepts that intentionally lack UC anchors.

**Rules verifier:** The graph is a clean DAG: no cycles, every slug named in a requires edge is defined, and there are zero redundant transitive edges. The three multi-parent nodes were designed to avoid transitive noise correctly — phys_em [phys_mech, calc_2] and engr_circuits [phys_em, diff_eq] both deliberately omit the redundant lower-calc edges (the notes explicitly say the calc chain is 'inherited, not duplicated'), and human_physiology [bio_cell_molec, gen_chem_1] joins two independent sources. So criteria (c) and (d) are fully clean.  The substantive problems are all in criterion (a) real-world sanity, concentrated in the math spine. linear_alg->calc_3 and diff_eq->linear_alg are the two edges that are genuinely non-standard at CA community colleges (modal prereqs are calc_2 for both), and because engr_circuits sits on top of diff_eq they compound into the graph's deepest and least defensible chain, asserting that intro circuits requires completed multivariable calculus and linear algebra. The diff_eq->linear_alg edge additionally contradicts the vocabulary's own observation that ~20 colleges teach the two as one combined coreq course. The notes are commendably transparent — every over-chain is flagged as a deliberate conservative/normative call — so nothing here is hidden, but 'documented' is not the same as 'genuinely standard,' and this audit's job is to flag that these edges will inflate complexity metrics well above what any CA CC actually requires. Secondary issues: an uneven tie-breaking philosophy (strictest-edge for math vs modal-loosest for comp_arch/digital_logic), a systemic coreq-as-prereq upgrade across physics/engr/discrete, and a couple of missing normative notes (discrete_math sub-calc_1 gating, world_lit prereq strength). Recommend, at minimum, relaxing linear_alg to calc_2 and reconsidering the diff_eq->linear_alg ordering (or documenting it as a coreq-collapsed edge), since those two changes remove the bulk of the unjustified depth.
